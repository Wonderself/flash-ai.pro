The Accords

Three frameworks. One position.

Why AI CAT, the Isaac Accords and the Abraham Accords matter to your AI strategy — and how we work along them.

AI infrastructure is no longer just a technical choice — it is a geopolitical one. Where your models run, whose ecosystems your partners belong to, and which cooperation frameworks protect your supply chain now shape cost, compliance and access to talent.

We position our work deliberately along three frameworks that are redrawing the map. We do not speak for any government — we read these agreements as operators, and we build the commercial corridors they open.

AI CAT Agreement — United States ✕ Israel

AI COOPERATION

The US–Israel AI cooperation track aligns two of the densest AI ecosystems on earth on research, standards and deployment. For our clients, the practical meaning is access: architectures and practices validated across both ecosystems, and partner firms working at that intersection.

What it means for you: a deployment designed to be compatible with where enterprise AI standards are actually being set.

Isaac Accords — Israel ✕ Argentina

AI · CYBER · SIGNED MOUs

A new cooperation framework with signed memoranda covering AI collaboration and security cooperation. We are careful about what it is: a positioning and cooperation framework — not a visa program, not a bilateral fund. Its value is the corridor it opens between the Israeli ecosystem and Latin America.

What it means for you: if you operate in or toward LATAM, we bring Israeli-grade AI and security practice into that corridor — in Spanish, with local compliance (Ley 25.326) built in.

Abraham Accords — the regional corridor

AI · CYBER · NORMALISATION

The normalisation framework that turned the region into a live technology corridor. Its AI and cybersecurity dimension keeps expanding — joint ventures, shared research, cross-border deployments between Israel and the Gulf.

What it means for you: access to a wider regional network of capital, infrastructure and partnerships through our position inside the Israeli ecosystem.
